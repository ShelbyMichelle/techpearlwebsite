What is Cervical Cancer?

Cervical cancer occurs in the cells of the cervix — the lower part of the uterus that connects to the vagina. It’s mainly caused by the Human Papillomavirus (HPV), which spreads through sexual contact.

  • Early stages may have no symptoms.
  • Later symptoms include unusual bleeding, pain during sex, and discharge.

Prevention

  • Get vaccinated against HPV — the vaccine is available in Malawi for girls aged 9–13.
  • Use condoms to reduce the risk of HPV transmission.
  • Avoid smoking and multiple sexual partners.

Screening & Treatment

Regular screening can detect precancerous changes early. The two main tests used in Malawi include:

  • VIA (Visual Inspection with Acetic acid): A quick and affordable screening test.
  • Pap smear: A test to check for changes in cervical cells.

Treatment options depend on the stage and include cryotherapy, surgery, radiotherapy, and chemotherapy.

Where to Get Help in Malawi

  • Queen Elizabeth Central Hospital — Blantyre
  • Kamuzu Central Hospital — Lilongwe
  • Zomba Central Hospital — Zomba
  • Local health centres offer VIA screening for free.

Talk to a nurse or doctor at your nearest clinic for screening or vaccination details.

Quick Facts

  • Cervical cancer is preventable and treatable if detected early.
  • HPV vaccine offers strong protection.
  • Regular screening saves lives.
